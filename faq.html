Frequently Asked Questions (Faq)

Kabataan's Frequently Asked Questions on Mental Health is a resource that provides information on mental health and mental illness. It covers topics such as mental health, what causes mental illness, and how to get help.

Mental Health

Mental health refers to our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It is how we think, feel, and act to cope with life. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices.

Some common mental health disorders include anxiety disorders, depression, and bipolar disorder.

There are many things you can do to improve your mental health. You can exercise regularly, eat a healthy diet, get enough sleep, and avoid drugs and alcohol. You can also try relaxation techniques such as yoga or meditation.

If you think you have a mental health disorder, you should see a mental health professional for an evaluation. They can help you get a diagnosis and develop a treatment plan.

Indications of a mental health disorder can vary depending on the specific condition. However, some common signs include feeling sad or down, having trouble sleeping, feeling anxious or stressed, having difficulty concentrating, and feeling hopeless or helpless.

Mental health disorders can be treated but usually cannot be cured. Most people with mental health disorders can improve their symptoms and live productive lives with treatment.

There is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question. The most effective treatment for a mental health disorder depends on the specific condition and the individual. However, treatment usually includes medication and therapy.

If you know someone with a mental health disorder, the best thing you can do is be supportive. Offer to listen to them and help them find resources if they need them. You can also help them by being understanding and patient.

There is no sure way to prevent mental health disorders. However, you can lower your risk by taking steps to promote good mental health. This includes regular exercise, eating a healthy diet, getting enough sleep, and managing stress.

If you think someone you know is suicidal, the best thing to do is to talk to them about it. Let them know that you are worried about them and offer to help them get help. You can also call a suicide hotline in your area.

The causes of mental health disorders are not fully understood but may be due to biological, psychological, and social factors.

Mental health disorders are typically diagnosed by a mental health professional, such as a psychiatrist, using criteria from the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM).

Mental health disorders can be treated with various methods, including medication, psychotherapy, and support groups.

The long-term effects of mental health disorders can vary depending on the specific condition but may include problems with employment, relationships, and housing.

If you are concerned about someone you think may have a mental health disorder, the best thing to do is talk to them about your concerns and encourage them to seek professional help.

If you think you may have a mental health disorder, it is vital to seek professional help. A mental health professional can provide a diagnosis and recommend treatment options.

There are many resources available to learn more about mental health disorders. Some helpful resources include the Department of Health (DOH) - Mental Health Program.

Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety disorders are a type of mental illness that can cause significant distress and impairment in daily functioning. There are various types of anxiety disorders, each with its symptoms.

Symptoms of anxiety disorders can vary depending on the specific disorder but may include persistent and excessive worry, fear, or anxiety; avoidance of certain situations or activities; and physical symptoms such as trembling, rapid heart rate, and sweating.

The exact cause of anxiety disorders is not known, but they are thought to be a combination of genetic and environmental factors.

Anxiety disorders are usually diagnosed based on a person’s symptoms. A mental health professional will conduct a clinical interview and may also use psychological questionnaires or rating scales to assess a person’s symptoms.

The most common treatment for anxiety disorders is psychotherapy, which can be combined with medication in some cases. Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) is a type of psychotherapy often used to treat anxiety disorders and has been found to be effective.

With treatment, the prognosis for people with anxiety disorders is generally reasonable. However, without treatment, anxiety disorders can harm a person’s life, causing significant distress and impairment in functioning.

There is no cure for anxiety disorders, but with treatment, most people can manage their symptoms and live relatively everyday lives.

Anxiety disorders are the most common mental illness, affecting millions worldwide.

If you think you may have an anxiety disorder, you should see a mental health professional for an evaluation.

Many resources are available for people with anxiety disorders and their loved ones. The National Center For Mental Health is an excellent place to start.

Depression

Depression is a mental disorder characterized by low mood, loss of interest in activities, and low self-esteem.

Symptoms of depression can include low mood, loss of interest in activities, low self-esteem, fatigue, changes in appetite, sleep problems, and difficulty concentrating.

There is no single cause of depression, but it is thought to be caused by a combination of genetic, biological, psychological, and environmental factors.

Treatment for depression can include medication, therapy, and lifestyle changes.

If you are worried about someone you know who may be depressed, the best thing to do is to talk to them about it. You can also offer to help them get treatment if they are willing to seek it.

If you are feeling depressed, the best thing to do is to talk to your doctor or mental health professional. They can help you get the treatment you need.

The outlook for people with depression varies depending on the severity of the condition. With treatment, most people with depression can improve their symptoms.

There is no sure way to prevent depression, but there are things you can do to reduce your risk, such as getting regular exercise, eating a healthy diet, and getting enough sleep.

Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder, also known as manic-depressive illness, is a brain disorder that causes unusual shifts in mood, energy, activity levels, and the ability to carry out day-to-day tasks.

The symptoms of bipolar disorder can be divided into three categories: mania, depression, and mixed. Mania is characterized by abnormally high energy levels, elated mood, racing thoughts, and decreased need for sleep. Depression is characterized by low energy levels, a sad mood, decreased interest in activities, and increased demand for rest. Mixed features refer to a state in which both mania and depression are present simultaneously.

The cause of the bipolar disorder is not fully understood, but it is thought to be a combination of genetic and environmental factors.

Bipolar disorder is typically diagnosed by a mental health professional based on a detailed clinical assessment.

Bipolar disorder is typically treated with a combination of medication and psychotherapy.

The long-term outlook for people with bipolar disorder is generally positive, but the condition can be recurrent and lead to significant impairment in some individuals.

Bipolar disorder can lead to several risks and complications, including suicide, substance abuse, and financial problems.

There is no known way to prevent bipolar disorder, but early diagnosis and treatment can help reduce the severity and frequency of episodes.

If you think you or someone you know has bipolar disorder, it is essential to seek professional help.

Stress

Stress is the body's response to any demand placed upon it. Stress can be caused by various factors, including physical, emotional, environmental, and psychological factors.

Stress can affect mental health by causing anxiety, depression, and other mental health disorders. Stress can also worsen existing mental health disorders.

There are various ways to manage stress, including exercise, relaxation techniques, and counseling.

Stress includes irritability, difficulty concentrating, headaches, and muscle tension.

Consequences of stress can include physical health problems, such as high blood pressure and heart disease, and mental health problems, such as anxiety and depression.

There are various ways to prevent stress, including exercise, relaxation techniques, and counseling.

If you think you're experiencing stress, you should see a doctor or mental health professional to discuss your symptoms and develop a plan to manage your stress.

If you have a stress attack, you should try to relax and calm yourself. You can try deep breathing exercises, visualization, or other relaxation techniques. You should see a doctor or mental health professional if you're still feeling stressed.

Eating Disorders

There are four main eating disorders: anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, binge-eating disorder, and eating disorders not otherwise specified (EDNOS).

The symptoms of an eating disorder vary depending on the type of disorder, but can include preoccupation with food, weight, and body image; Extreme weight loss or weight gain; Skipping meals; Eating in secret; Feelings of shame or guilt around food; Extreme exercise; Purging behaviors (vomiting, excessive use of laxatives or diuretics); and Irregular menstrual cycles.

The cause of eating disorders is not fully understood but is thought to be a combination of genetic, psychological, and environmental factors.

Eating disorders can profoundly affect mental health, leading to anxiety, depression, and even suicide.

Treatment for eating disorders typically includes a combination of psychotherapy, nutritional counseling, and medication.

Eating disorders can lead to serious health complications, such as heart problems, kidney failure, and bone loss. They can also lead to death.

There is no sure way to prevent an eating disorder from developing. However, there are some things you can do to reduce your risk, such as maintaining healthy body weight, eating a balanced diet, and getting regular exercise.

If you think you or someone you know has an eating disorder, it is important to seek professional help. Eating disorders are serious mental illnesses that require treatment.

Many resources are available for someone with an eating disorder, including support groups, counseling, and treatment centers.

Many resources are available online and in libraries that provide more information about eating disorders.

Self-Harm

Self-harm is the act of intentionally harming oneself, usually to deal with difficult emotions or situations.

The signs and symptoms of self-harm can vary, including cutting or burning oneself, pulling out hair, or self-poisoning.

Anyone can be at risk for self-harm, but it is more common in people with a history of mental health problems, trauma, or abuse.

The consequences of self-harm can be physical, emotional, and mental. Self-harm can lead to infection, scarring, and even death. It can also increase the risk of future mental health problems.

If you are self-harming, it is essential to get help from a mental health professional. You can also call a crisis hotline for support.

Youth Mental Health

Youth mental health refers to the mental health and wellbeing of young people between 13 and 30. It encompasses a range of topics, including mental disorders, emotional difficulties, and social and behavioral problems.

Young people's most common mental health disorders include anxiety disorders, depression, conduct disorders, and substance abuse disorders.

The signs and symptoms of a mental health disorder in young people can vary depending on the specific disorder. However, common signs and symptoms include changes in mood, sleep patterns, appetite, energy levels, concentration, and behavior.

There are several risk factors for mental health disorders in young people, including family history, stressful life events, bullying, and exposure to violence.

There is no sure way to prevent mental health disorders in young people. However, several things can help reduce the risk, such as maintaining a healthy lifestyle, building positive relationships, and getting help early if problems arise.

The treatment of mental health disorders in young people depends on the specific disorder. Treatment may include medication, psychotherapy, and lifestyle changes.

The long-term effects of mental health disorders in young people can vary depending on the specific disorder. However, common effects include problems with school, work, and relationships, as well as an increased risk of substance abuse and suicide.

Several resources are available for more information about youth mental health, including books, websites, and support groups.

How to Get Help

If you are experiencing any of the following symptoms, it may be time to seek help from a mental health professional:

- Excessive worry or anxiety
- Depression or feelings of hopelessness
- Difficulty concentrating
- Changes in eating or sleeping habits
- Withdrawal from friends or activities
- Extreme mood swings
- Thoughts of harming yourself or others

If you are in the Philippines, you can call the DOH-NCMH 24/7 Crisis Hotline:​ 0917-899-8727 to speak to a trained counselor. If you are in another country, you can visit the International Association for Suicide Prevention website at https://www.iasp.info/ for a list of crisis centers worldwide.

When you seek help from a mental health professional, they will likely ask you about your symptoms, thoughts, and feelings. They may also ask about your family history and current stressors. This information will help them to make a diagnosis and develop a treatment plan. Treatment may include medication, therapy, and lifestyle changes.

There are many ways to get help if you are unable to afford it. In the Philippines, you can call the DOH-NCMH 24/7 Crisis Hotline:​ 0917-899-8727 to find free or low-cost mental health services. You can also visit the website of the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration at https://doh.gov.ph/national-mental-health-program to find treatment facilities near you.

In the Philippines, mental health treatment is confidential. Your mental health provider cannot share your information without your consent. There are some exceptions to this rule, such as if you are a danger to yourself or others.

It's important to remember that mental health conditions are real and are nothing to be ashamed of. Mental health professionals are there to help you, not to judge you.

Therapy is not a one-size-fits-all approach. There are many different types of therapy, and you may need to try a few different types before you find one that works for you. It's also important to find a therapist that you feel comfortable with. If you don't feel like therapy is working, talk to your therapist about it. They may be able to make some adjustments to your treatment plan.

Not everyone needs medication to treat their mental health condition. However, for some people, medication is an important part of treatment. If you are considering medication, talk to your mental health professional about the risks and benefits.

It's important to remember that mental health conditions are treatable. With treatment, you can feel better and live a fulfilling life.

If you want to learn more about mental health, many resources are available. In the Philippines, you can visit the National Center for Mental Health website at https://ncmh.gov.ph/ or the Department of Health (DOH) website at https://doh.gov.ph/press-release/launch-of-the-NCMH-crisis-hotline.
