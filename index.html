About Kabataan.ph

Kabataan.ph is a website that provides information and resources about mental health and wellbeing. The website aims to help people understand and manage their mental health and to provide support and information to those who care for someone with a mental health problem.

What is Mental Health?

Mental health refers to our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It is how we think, feel, and act to cope with life. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices.

OUR CAMPAIGNS

The #40SecondsOfHope, #VoicesOfHope, #UsapTayo, and #KabataanTalks campaigns are all youth mental health campaigns that aim to raise awareness about mental health and provide support to those struggling with mental illness. These campaigns provide a platform for young people to share their stories and offer hope and support to others.

#40SecondsOfHope

The #40SecondsOfHope campaign encourages young people to take action and get help if they feel suicidal. The campaign provides information on how to get help and support someone who is suicidal. The campaign also encourages young people to talk about their mental health and seek help if they struggle.

#VoicesOfHope

The #VoicesofHope campaign is a youth mental health campaign that aims to provide support and resources to young people struggling with mental health. The campaign offers a platform for young people to share their stories and experiences and to connect with others who are going through similar struggles. The campaign also offers information and support on various topics related to mental health and provides a space for open discussion and dialogue about mental health.

#UsapTayo

#UsapTayo is a Youth Mental Health Campaign aimed at promoting mental health and wellbeing among young people in the Philippines. The campaign encourages young people to talk about their mental health, seek help if they are struggling, and support each other.

#KabataanTalks

KabataanTalks is a national youth-led mental health campaign in the Philippines that aims to break the stigma around mental health and encourage open discussion about mental health issues among young people. The campaign provides a platform for young people to share their stories and experiences with mental health and to connect with others who can relate to their struggles. KabataanTalks also provides resources and information on mental health and offers support to young people struggling with mental health.

How to cope with feeling lonely at school

If you're feeling lonely at school, you can do a few things to cope. First, try to find a club or activity you're interested in. This will help you make friends who share your interests. You can also try talking to your classmates and getting to know them better. Finally, talk to a teacher or counselor if you're still feeling lonely. They can help you find ways to make friends and feel less alone.

Len Marjorie Sy

How I learnt to show up for myself like I show up for others

I realized that I needed to start showing up for myself the way I show up for others. I started by making time for myself every day, even if it was just 10 minutes. It wasn't always easy, but I slowly started to feel better and more like myself again. I also began to say no to things that didn't serve me and to make my wellbeing a priority.

The validation of self-harm scars among the mental health community

The validation of self-harm scars in the mental health community is a necessary process that allows individuals who self-harm to feel understood and supported. Validating self-harm scars can help individuals feel more comfortable in their own skin and encourage them to seek help for their mental health needs.
