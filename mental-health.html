ABOUT MENTAL HEALTH

It is estimated that one in four people worldwide will be affected by mental or neurological disorders at some point in their lives. Mental disorders are widespread, with nearly one in five adults experiencing a mental illness in a year.

Mental health is essential to our overall well-being. It is the foundation of our ability to think, feel, and behave in ways that enable us to lead productive, satisfying lives and relate to others. Mental health is a state of well-being in which an individual realizes their abilities, can cope with the normal stresses of life, can work productively and fruitfully, and can contribute to their community.

The World Health Organization (WHO) defines mental health as "a state of well-being in which every individual realizes his or her potential, can cope with the normal stresses of life, can work productively and fruitfully, and can contribute to his or her community."

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices.

Good mental health is not just the absence of mental health problems. It is also the ability to recover from or manage difficult life experiences and to grow and learn from them.

Mental health is essential at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood. Over the course of your life, if you experience mental health problems, your thinking, mood, and behavior could be affected.

Mental health problems can range from mild to severe and can be short-lived or long-lasting. They may arise from a complex interaction of genetic, biological, psychological, social, and cultural factors.

Mental health problems are common, but help is available. If you are experiencing a mental health problem, many people can help you.

The first step is to talk to someone who can help. This could be a friend, family member, doctor, therapist, or counselor.

If you are feeling suicidal or harming yourself, please call the National Center for Mental Health Crisis Hotline at 0966-351-4518.

There are many types of mental health conditions, including:

  • Anxiety disorders
  • Depressive disorders
  • Bipolar disorder
  • Eating disorders
  • Personality disorders
  • Schizophrenia
  • Substance abuse disorders

Mental health problems can also be caused by physical health problems, such as:

  • Brain injuries
  • Dementia
  • Stroke

Mental health problems can also be caused by:

  • Traumatic experiences, such as abuse, neglect, or violence
  • Poverty
  • Unemployment
  • Social isolation

Mental health problems are treatable. People with mental health problems can recover and live whole and productive lives with treatment.

There are many effective treatments for mental health conditions. These include:

  • Psychotherapy
  • Medication
  • Support groups
  • Self-care
